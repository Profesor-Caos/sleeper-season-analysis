🏅 The Winner's Circle

🏆 - Connor - Team CeeLoGreen97 - (13-1)

A big congratulations to our champion @CeeLoGreen97!

Conor went 13-1 in the regular season and continued the dominance into the postseason by taking home the championship trophy.

If it's any consolation for the rest of us, this is dynasty, and Connor did draft the oldest team.

Those veterans paid off big time, by scoring the most points in the league by far - with his average points scored exceeding the league average by almost 22%.

Lamar, Baker, and Saquon combined to annihilate the competition, proving the importance of Quarterbacks in this Superflex format.

His road through the regular season was also very favorable, getting to face our bottom two teams both twice (sorry Gwen and Ira).

Against other teams, his opponents on average scored 4.5% lower than the league average, a number brought down both by getting to face Gwen and Ira each twice, but also by not having to face himself (normalizing against league averages definitely makes a difference when all the other teams had Connor dropping a stupid amount of points on them).

Facing his mighty squad had his opponents shaking in their boots. - Despite his opponents scoring just 4.5% under the league average against other opponents, when against Connor, those same teams scored almost 8.6% lower than the league average, underperforming their expectations big time.

We'll see if he can repeat his ridiculous performance next year.

🥈 - Ryan W - Dan Flashes your Nabers - (8-6)

A round of applause for our runner up! @rynoceros17 had a strong finish after a rocky 2-4 start to the season.

Making the playoffs was a close call, ending the last 4 games of the season with a scary 2 losses in a row, then finishing strong with 2 wins to clinch it.

Ryan's was another team that had a fairly lucky run into the playoffs, facing about an average strength of schedule, but having teams underperform their expectations by about 5%, allowing a team that scored in the bottom half of the league in points for to sneak into the playoffs.

But that's not where Ryan's story ended - a couple wins in the playoffs, and he brought home silver. You never know what's going to happen in Fantasy! Patrick Mahomes was a strong QB leader for the team, and RB De'Von Achane scored big, including in the playoffs when it mattered - while rookies Bucky Irving and Malik Nabers shone bright.

🥉 - Abe - Team SgtWeenieArms - (8-6)

And @SgtWeenieArms gets the bronze! Abe had a solid squad that scored the 3rd most points.

While his strength of schedule was fairly middling, scoring the 7th most points, against Abe, those teams overperformed by 2%, resulting in the 5th most points scored against him.

The most impressive part about Abe's season was the turnaround. After starter 1-4 and then 2-6, Abe won 6 straight games to finish the season with one of the best records in the league.

Josh Allen and Justin Herbert proved their QB talent, while Taylor Swift's boyfriend continuing to put up decent points and a mid-season trade for Drake London helped secure a playoff spot after that unfortunate start.

🎉 The Honorable Mentions

🎯 Sera - Dynasty Gourmand - (9-5)

@PrincessofRaven finished the regular season with the 2nd strongest record at 9-5, and taking the 4th place finish in the playoffs.

Interestingly, while she had the 2nd best record, her points for during the regular season were 3rd lowest in the league. So certainly, her strength of schedule must have been a cakewalk, right?

I thought that would be the case as well, but she faced the hardest strength of schedule in the league, facing both Abe and Josh twice.

Against her, teams underperformed their average performances by 8.8%, showing the incredible variance that can happen in Fantasy Football.

Jalen Hurts led the effort, hindered by an injured Rashee Rice and Brandon Aiyuk, while James Conner, Aaron Jones, and Courtland Sutton provided consistent points.

💪 Josh - Joshi - (8-6)

@yoahiwa had a dominant regular season, scoring the 2nd most points in the league and handing Connor his only loss all year.

Unfortunately for Josh, while the schedule was not overly difficult, teams overperformed and he faced the 3rd most points against.

Sam Darnold was a surprising points leader for the squad, while Derick Henry and Amon-Ra St. Brown put up more expected but nonetheless powerful performances.

⚡ Bryce - Mormen less problems - (8-6)

@jazzyjackalope led an unlucky squad that scored 5th most in the league to a solid 8-6 record against the most points scored by opponents.

While the strength of schedule was very average, Bryce's opponents outperformed their expected scores by over 9%.

After starting out with a strong 4-0 record, the squad had a tougher time, without a winning or losing streak longer than 2, to end up finishing 4-6

Bo Nix performed well as a rookie, while Bijan Robinson, James Cook, and Jonathon Taylor were a terrific RB trio. Maybe next season you should play better defense 😛

⚖️ Those Who Were Mid

🤷 Gabe - Godo(n)t stop me - (7-7)

After a blazing 6-2 start, @EmpiricalFool must have made a coaching change or something (sorry Gabe), suffering heartbreaking defeat after heartbreaking defeat as the team collapsed to a 7-7 final record.

The team was among the most statistically average in the league, almost even in points for and points again, with a moderately difficult strength of schedule that ended up underperforming against him.

Baker Mayfield led the team, but was traded to CeeLoGreen97 in the playoffs. Jahmyr Gibbs and Kyren Williams were some of the league's top RBs.

Ladd McConkey showed a lot of promise as a young WR, and Michael Penix hopes to be a strong QB for the Falcons' future.

🕳️ Chelsea - Supermassive Black Hole - (7-7)

The other team that finished 7-7, @xXTeamJacobXx similarly had a hot 5-2 start, putting up wins against some of the easier teams in the league.

However, the squad faded down the stretch, losing in valiant efforts against some of the best teams.

The team was also very statistically average, averaging just 0.0016 points per game shy of the league average. The strength of schedule was around the most average, with opponents performing about as expected.

Joe Burrow was a standout leader on the roster, and AJ Brown continued to be a reliable WR1 (Wide Receiver 1, i.e. a top 12 WR). Hopefully, Marvin Harrison lives up to his expectations in the coming season.

😤 Conor - The Wright Stuff - (6-8)

It was a difficult season for @TwiceMarked, as what was a solid team all around that started 5-3, slid to a sad 6-8 record.

It would seem the 4th highest scoring team should have done better than 6-8, but Conor fell on the wrong side of variance as his points were just barely insufficient too many times (I really should have done some margin of victory/defeat analysis too. Maybe next year).

Jared Goff, Justin Jefferson, and Kyler Murray all had good years as expected, while Terry McLaurin had a career year with the best QB he's ever had throwing him the ball.

Finally, we'll see if JJ McCarthy will step up as the Vikings QB next year.

🪦

🤕 Ryan G - Team ProfesorCaos - (5-9)

At 5-9, I wasn't sure if I should consider myself a team who missed the playoffs or this category, but then I recalled how my season went.

Although I finished 5-9, I started 0-4 and at one point was 2-8 - certainly outside of playoff contention.

Statistically, I did score around the same range of points as teams that made or just missed the playoffs - 7th most points scored.

While my strength of schedule was very average, teams scored over 6.3% more than expected against me, resulting in having the 2nd most points against me. Looks like I need to learn how to play defense too.

I certainly made the most trades in the season, after Tua suffered a concussion early in the season and my RB depth proved lackluster, I decided it wasn't my year. Alas, Ja'Marr Chase's career year was wasted. Puka Nacua continued to be great when healthy, and rookie Caleb Williams looked okay as Bears fans hope he becomes a top QB soon.

🐣 Gwen - ZZ Bottom - (4-10)

A team that started out as a rebuilding squad, @dontwearsandals drafted a lot of young players, many of whom hit big in their rookie season.

Don't let the 4-10 record fool you - this team should have been a lot worse than that.

Despite having the fewest points scored in the league, a favorable strength of schedule and opponents underperforming by over 4.5% propelled this young band of up-and-comers to a better record than expected.

But while the record was bad this year, look out for the future. CeeDee Lamb gave veteran leadership as youngsters Jayden Daniels, Brock Bowers, and Brian Thomas Jr. all exploded onto the scene. Gwen's team can be expected to be much stronger next year.

💩 Ira - Carradine Curtain Cords - (1-13)

It was a rough season for @RustySporxxx, as his first round pick, Christian McCaffrey, missed the first half of the season, and didn't even play 4 games all year.

Unfortunately, the lineup was set rather inconsistently, and a lot of potential points were left on the bench throughout the year.

Ira's points for were 2nd lowest in the league, but he faced more points scored against him than Gwen, granting him the bottom position on the season.

There's still a lot of promise on the roster, as Trevor Lawrence got a new WR drafted 2nd overall in this year's draft, and the young WR trio of Zay Flowers, George Pickens, and Jaxon Smith-Njigba will be good for years to come.
